Starting Fresh

Having allergies or caring for people with allergies is not an easy feat, especially to start. What I have found helpful is to do a little “spring cleaning” except in your pantry. It can appear daunting, so you can either do it little by little, or in one fell swoop. The good news (if there’s such a thing) with food allergies is that it forces you to adopt new eating habits, so this “spring cleaning” event provides you an opportunity to eat healthier as well.

The first step in creating an Allergy Free Pantry is to get rid of as much allergens and unhealthy food as possible.

Here are a few tips to get started:

  • Read ingredient labels; a rule of thumb I follow is that there should be a minimal number of ingredients, and everything on the label should be recognizable.

  • Avoid foods with high fructose corn syrup, artificial colors, artificial flavors, natural flavors (these still contain a lot of chemicals), hydrogenated or partially hydrogenated oils, wheat flour (this is the same as white flour; look for whole wheat flour or white whole wheat flour).

  • Also, ingredients are listed in order of dominance, so if sugar is listed first, that is the most predominant ingredient. So ditch the processed food (cookies, cereals, soda, chips, and artificially flavored drinks) and opt for a healthier option.

The next step is to refill the empty shelves with unprocessed, whole foods. Once you have a few of the basics stocked, you will find that it becomes easier to cook healthier, allergy friendly meals.

Here are some of the ingredients that I try to keep in stock:

Fresh Produce

  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Carrots
  • Celery
  • Fresh Vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower, peppers, cabbage, kale, spinach, potatoes)
  • Fresh Fruit (bananas for smoothies, whatever is in season)
  • Fresh parsley, green onions, cilantro
  • Frozen Furit for smoothies

Pantry Items

  • Whole grains (brown rice, oats, barley, quinoa, wild rice)
  • Whole grain pasta *gluten-free varieties
  • Canned or dried beans (black beans, kidney beans, red beans)
  • Boxed organic vegetable and chicken broth
  • Canned tomatoes (chopped or crushed)
  • Tomato Paste
  • Oils (sunflowerseed oil, extra virgin olive oil, coconut oil)
  • Vinegars (red wine vinegar, balsamic vinegar)
  • Sea Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Spices (my favorites include: cinnamon, cumin, chili powder, oregano, garlic powder, crushed red pepper, curry)
  • Pure Vanilla Extract
