About Me

Hello, my name is Katie Lippe and this is my attempt to give you a little glimpse of my life currently and sneak peak into the complicated world of allergens. To start, I grew up in the bustling town(!?) of Fredonia Wisconsin - population 2,160. I graduated from UW-Eau Claire in 2016 with a degree in psychology and I'm currently attending graduate school at UW-Stout. In my free time, I enjoy running in marathons (not training) and coaching youth figure skating in Eau Claire Wisconsin, a sport I grew up competing in my whole life.

Unfortunately, I've struggled with stomach issues for most of my life and they have become increasingly worse over the past few years. Most of my doctor appointements and ER visits left me incredibly frustrated and confused because it felt like no one was willing to take the time and effort to diagnose the internal issues I was dealing with.

I finally found a doctor that took time to listen to my symptoms and commit to helping me find an answer. After undergone countless blood tests and a variety of others, I finally was given an answer. The official diagnoses confirm that I have Eosinophilic Esophagitis and Eosinophilic Colitis (complicated terms saying that I have a build up of allergy cells in my esophagus). Despite the uneasiness of knowing you have two diseases, it was a great relief to finaly have something to move forward with. After being tested for food and environmental allergies, I learned that I was allergic to the following foods: Gluten, Almonds, Hazelnuts, Peanuts, Pistachios, Walnuts, Dairy, Eggs, Coconut, and Corn.

Oddly enough, trying to explain my allergies and diseases to people brings me an unusual amount of anxiety; however, I've slowly learned that it simply has become the "new normal" and is not worth being embarassed about. Allergies also come with its fair share of challenges which is why I wanted to create this blog. Struggling with these constant challenges can be extremely difficult, so I hope that my blog can provide you bits of information that eases the daily challenges.
